## Analysis

CALLED at R3/T70+ on structural grounds. HEADLINE: the design intent Div>Abj HOLDS - Divination controls the exhaustion war from every angle (Saboteur mills put Abj 6 cards ahead on the clock, Sealed Vault reset Div's own cycle for FREE at deck-4 while Abj took exhaustion #1, and Div's selection keeps quality up). Abj hit reshuffle #1 first; Div stays a cycle behind indefinitely. Projected result: Div wins the escalation race or the turn-limit HP comparison. BUT the matchup exposed three degeneracies, in severity order: (1) INFINITE ROUND - rounds only end when a player exhausts SLOTS by casting; two passive schools just... don't cast. This game sat in ROUND 3 for 60+ turns. The round needs a secondary end condition (e.g., round ends after each wizard has taken N turns, or a forced action rule). (2) EXHAUSTION IS TOO SOAKABLE - wards absorb the 2,4,6 ticks (Abj took #1 at zero net HP), so lethal cumulative exhaustion needs ~5-6 cycles = hundreds of turns. Exhaustion should BYPASS wards (it is internal strain, not an attack) and/or escalate steeper. (3) NO DAMAGE AXIS AT ALL in this pairing - Abj literally cannot deal damage to a school that never attacks (Reflective only fires on absorption), and Div has no damage below L3+. That is acceptable IF exhaustion is a real clock - fixing #2 fixes #3. Also observed: 39-card hands (no hand cap) - ugly but not load-bearing. SECONDARY (high confidence, not piloted): Evo>Div is safe - Div cannot interact with burn or direct damage and dies ~R4-5 like pre-nerf Abj; piloting it is due diligence for a future session, not a doubt. TRIANGLE STATUS after this session: Evo vs Abj = competitive (fixed via Kindle nerf); Div>Abj = holds directionally but needs the exhaustion/round-end fixes to be playable; Evo>Div = almost certainly holds.
