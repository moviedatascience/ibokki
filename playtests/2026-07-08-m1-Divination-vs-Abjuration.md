## Analysis

## Purpose

Re-pilot of m4 (Divination P0 vs Abjuration P1, **seed 4401**, both sides piloted) at Omen-2, with the three 2026-07-08 engine fixes LIVE, to (a) verify each fix behaves correctly in real play and (b) confirm no balance regression. CALLED at R4 prepare — Div 28 / Abj 26 after three fully piloted rounds.

## FIX VERIFICATIONS (primary deliverable) — 2 of 3 exercised live, all pass

**1. Runic Seal interactive target picker (ABJ-010) — VERIFIED LIVE.** R3T9, Abj's lead window: cast Runic Seal (SS). On resolution the engine PAUSED and presented an interactive choice — "Seal target prepared spell" — offering ALL FOUR of Div's uncast prepared spells (Omen/Foretell/Anticipate/Insight), shown by real defId (they were face-up from prior-round casts). The old SIMPLIFIED code auto-seized the first uncast slot with no choice. Picked Omen; it immediately showed `(SEALED)` and had NO cast option for Div for the rest of the round (Div could still attach fuel but not cast). The pick correctly locked the CHOSEN slot, not slot 0 by accident.

**2. Arcane Shell round-scoped draw-rider (ABJ-002) — VERIFIED LIVE.** Abj cast Arcane Shell on R2T8; its 1-HP ward persisted across the round boundary into R3 (wards [1] at R3 start). On R3T10 a Div Foretell destroyed that carried-over Shell: Abj took the 1 spill (27->26) but **drew NOTHING — hand stayed at 1, deck at 30**. Under the pre-fix bug the cross-round `onDestroy: draw2` rider would have fired (hand->3, deck->28). The rider correctly lapsed at R2's end. This is the exact m4 live-bug ("rider paid across rounds twice"), now fixed and observed clean.

**3. Overclock 2-HP reprice (GAM-008) — NOT ENCOUNTERED.** Overclock is a Neutral gambit; neither archetype deck drew it through R3, so it could not be exercised in this match. It is covered by unit + gating tests (HP cost asserted; live-target requirement asserted). No action needed; flag for a future match that happens to draw it, or a deck-override spot-check if desired.

Bonus guard confirmations: Quenching Salts (anti-burn) was correctly NOT offered to Abj vs a burn-less Div (whiff-guard); the one-cast-per-turn rule and Fortify buff-vs-create both behaved correctly.

## Balance read — no regression; squarely on m4's target

Per-round face (originated): **R1** Div 1 (thorn), Abj 0 — identical to m4's fix-free baseline (confirms engine consistency; the three fixes don't perturb a round in which they don't fire). **R2** Div 3 (1 Foretell spill + 2 doom#2), Abj 0 -> Div +3 cume. **R3 (Seal round, Abj-led): EVEN** — the Seal denied the ~2 doom, and Abj's lone Ward Pulse thorn (1) matched Div's lone Foretell spill (1). Net after 3 rounds: **Div 28 / Abj 26, Div +2 (~0.67/round)** — inside m4's "slight-but-real Div edge / knife-edge" band, NOT a stomp. The intended soft-shape holds with the fixes in.

Notable: with the picker working, the Seal is a STRONGER, more honest tech than in m4 — Abj can target the highest-value threat (Omen) deliberately instead of hitting it only because it sat at prep index 0 (m4's explicit caveat: "Div could cheese prep order today"). That cheese is now closed. The Seal round demonstrated the alternating-round-type texture m4 predicted: on Abj's lead round the Seal deletes the clock in the free window before Div can re-fuel, and here it fully neutralized the round (even), at the cost of Abj's entire fuel round (only a bare Fortify + a WP thorn cast besides the Seal).

Also observed (known issue, not under test): Div hand-floods (reached 8, over cap) when its clock is sealed and it holds a card-rich hand with no damage outlet — consistent with the standing hand-flood finding.

## Caveats

Draws diverged slightly from m4 starting R2T6 (Abj drew CMP-S where m4 shows CMP-SM) — minor RNG/possible deck drift since m4 was logged; R1 was move- and draw-identical, and the strategic arc matched throughout, so the comparison is sound at the round/rate level. Both sides piloted; no mechanical/slug misplays (stable [slug] addressing eliminated the index-shift friction from cvc10/12/m4).

## Recommendation

Ship the three fixes. Seal picker and Shell rider are verified correct in live play and do not shift the Div-Abj read off m4's target; the Seal is now a cleaner costed-tech (prep-order cheese closed). Overclock's reprice remains test-covered only — verify opportunistically when a deck draws it. No further Div-Abj re-pilot needed for these fixes; the open balance frontier is unchanged (R5+ L2 doom cycle vs L2 walls — Abj's long-game win path, still unvalidated).
